# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Tech recruiters and hiring managers evaluating Ahmed Ismail for full-stack development roles. They land on the portfolio with a specific need: quickly assess technical range, quality of craft, and whether this developer fits their team's culture. They are typically short on time, comparing multiple candidates, and make snap judgments from the first few seconds of browsing.

## Product Purpose

A portfolio that functions as Ahmed Ismail's strongest credential — the site itself is the proof of capability. Every interaction, layout choice, and line of code should reinforce that this is a developer who cares about craft, thinks in systems, and delivers production-quality work. Success means a recruiter finishes browsing with a clear, confident impression of his skills and personality.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, confident, inventive. The voice is direct and assured without arrogance. The visual language is bold but refined — a developer who can handle the full stack and makes deliberate choices at every layer.

## Anti-references

- Corporate/generic templates of any kind. No sterile SaaS landing page patterns, no "modern agency" hero sections, no cookie-cutter card grids.
- The typical developer portfolio: terminal-only themes, plain white/blue gradients, over-reliance on open-source templates.
- Anything that feels loud for the sake of being noticed. The confidence should be earned by the work, not by gimmicks.
- The current cyberpunk direction should evolve away from tropes (scanlines, excessive neon glow, flickering animations) toward something more refined — keeping the dark/tech identity but with more restraint and polish.

## Design Principles

1. **Confidence without shouting** — The dark/tech identity stays, but refined. Power is felt in the precision, not in the volume of effects. Every animation, glow, and grid line earns its place.

2. **Craft as credential** — The portfolio's own execution is the first and best reference. Typography, spacing, motion, and responsiveness are immaculate because they are the work itself.

3. **Bilingual first** — English and Arabic are equal citizens. The layout, type system, and UX flow serve both directions without compromise. RTL is not an afterthought.

4. **Speed is a feature** — Fast loads, zero unnecessary JavaScript, smooth 60fps transitions. A developer portfolio that isn't performant undermines its own message.

5. **Every pixel intentional** — Nothing decorative. Each component, color, and interaction has a reason. If it doesn't serve clarity, credibility, or delight, it doesn't ship.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Reduced motion support: every animation must have a `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback, typically an instant reveal or crossfade.
- WCAG AA contrast as a baseline; body text against bg must hit 4.5:1.
- Keyboard-navigable throughout.
- Screen-reader friendly with semantic HTML and proper ARIA where needed.
